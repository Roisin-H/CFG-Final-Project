Anita Borg

Role:

American Computer Scientist

Bio:

Anita Borg from Illinois was a stellar computer scientist, gaining a PHD by New York University for her research investigating the synchronisation efficiency of operating systems.

In the late 80s, she turned her attention to the lack of female representation in her field and committed herself to challenge this by championing the advancement of women in technology through various ventures.

“Around the world, women are not full partners in driving the creation of new technology that will define their lives. This is not good for women and not good for the world…Women need to assume their rightful place at the table creating technology of the future.”

Main Achievement:

  • 1987 co-founded the Systers community, an email list for women working in the ’systems’ field and a safe community for women technologists.
  • 1994 founded the Institute for Women and Technology - aim to increase the representation of women in technical fields.
  • 1997 founded Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing - grown to be the worlds largest annual gathering of women technologists.

Why we love her

As a computer scientist and women activist, Anita Borg believed passionately in fighting for greater representation of women in tech.

She herself was not only a talented computer scientist but a pioneer of normalising women technologist, creating a safe community for which the ambitions of women in the workplace could thrive.

She is a mentor for the generations of women in tech to come.
