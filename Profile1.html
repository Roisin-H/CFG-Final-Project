Annie Easley

Computer scientist at NASA

Who is she?

Annie Jean Easley (1933-2011) was an African-American computer scientist, mathematician and rocket scientist at NASA, and its predecessor NACA. She was one of the first African-Americans to work at NASA, and contributed to developing software for the Centaur rocket stage.

Main Achievements

  • One of four African-Americans to work at NASA, in 1955.
  • One of the first women to wear pants to work in the 1970s.
  • Became an Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) counsellor at NASA, addressing discrimination complaints.
  • Her work on the Centaur contributed to the 1997 flight to Saturn of the Cassini probe.

Why we love her

Annie Easley is not only a woman, but she is a black woman. She succeeded and made a name for herself in the aeronautics field, despite facing the racism, segregation, and discrimination prevalent in USA before and during the Civil Rights Movement. She is a huge inspiration to women and people of colour worldwide, inspiring us to keep going after our goals, even if it feels like the whole world is against us.
